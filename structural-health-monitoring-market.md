<p><p>The Structural Health Monitoring (SHM) market is divided into two primary types: wired and wireless systems. Wired SHM systems utilize physical cables to connect sensors to data acquisition units, offering reliable data transmission and power supply. However, they can be costly and complicated to install. In contrast, wireless SHM systems use radio frequency technology to collect and transmit data, providing greater flexibility and ease of installation, though they may face challenges like signal interference and battery limitations.</p></p>
