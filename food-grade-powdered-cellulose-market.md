<p><p>The food-grade powdered cellulose market primarily consists of two types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based cellulose. Wood pulp-based cellulose is derived from the fibers of trees, making it a sustainable option widely used in food additives and texture modifiers. Refined cotton-based cellulose, sourced from cotton plants, is often utilized for its superior purity and fine texture, enhancing food consistency. Both types serve as bulking agents, stabilizers, and anti-caking agents, contributing to various food formulations and improving product quality.</p></p>
