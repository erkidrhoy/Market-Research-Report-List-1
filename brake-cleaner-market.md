<p><p>The brake cleaner market is divided into two primary types: chlorinated and non-chlorinated. Chlorinated brake cleaners contain solvents like trichloroethylene, which effectively dissolve grease and grime but may pose environmental and health risks. Non-chlorinated brake cleaners utilize paper or synthetic solvents, offering a safer alternative with reduced toxicity. Both types serve the automotive industry for cleaning brake components, ensuring optimal performance and safety, while their selection often depends on regulatory requirements, user preferences, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
